Getting started

What do I need to use node.dev?

An iPhone running node.dev, a Windows PC you want to reach, and a free Tailscale account signed in on both devices. The app walks you through the rest during onboarding.

Is node.dev available yet?

node.dev is launching on TestFlight beta and the App Store — coming soon. Email me if you'd like to be notified.

How do I set up my PC?

The full walkthrough is on the setup page: install Tailscale, run the setup script, pair your key, and add your host.

Do I need a Mac?

No. node.dev runs on your iPhone and connects to a Windows PC. Other server platforms may follow, but the current target is Windows.

Connection & Tailscale

Why does node.dev require Tailscale?

Tailscale gives you a private, encrypted link between your phone and PC without exposing your machine to the public internet. It is the safest way to reach your computer from anywhere, and it needs no port forwarding or static IP.

The key push failed. What now?

Make sure the PowerShell setup window is still open — it waits up to 10 minutes for your key. If you use custom Tailscale ACLs, confirm your phone can reach the PC on ports 22 and 9922. As a fallback, copy the public key from the app and paste it into the authorized_keys file the script created.

My connection dropped — did I lose my work?

No, if you're using tmux. Sessions are kept alive across dropped connections, locked phones, and reconnects. Reconnect and pick up where you left off. See the setup page for the optional WSL + tmux steps.

Security & privacy

Where are my SSH keys stored?

Your private key is generated on-device and stored in the iPhone's hardware-backed Keychain, locked behind Face ID / Touch ID. It is never uploaded, synced, or exported.

Does node.dev collect any data?

No. node.dev has no backend, no telemetry, and no analytics. Your keys, connection details, history, and work all stay on your devices. See the privacy policy.

Is it safe to run the setup script as Administrator?

Yes. It needs elevation to install the OpenSSH server and edit firewall rules. It locks SSH to your Tailscale network, enables key-only auth, never truncates existing keys, and is safe to re-run. You can download and read it before running.

Codie (AI agent)

What is Codie?

Codie is a chat-based AI coding agent that lives on your phone but runs on your machine. Describe what you want in plain language; Codie reads files, runs commands, makes edits, and works with git on your real project.

Can Codie do things without my approval?

You control its access per project — from read-only, to edit and git, to full autonomy. Codie asks for approval before sensitive actions, and each turn shows what it did, how long it took, and what it cost.

Is my code sent to the cloud?

Codie operates on your live project on your own machine. On supported iPhones, on-device Apple Intelligence makes Codie's voice sound natural — that processing stays on the device and is used only for conversation, never to send your code anywhere.
